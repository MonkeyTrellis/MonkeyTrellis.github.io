CONTEXT

Getting to Know the Company

Coming in after the seed phase, much of the company’s direction was already established. Getting a grasp on the history, the technology, and the people involved allowed me to take the next steps as a designer.

Studying the Styleguide

An in-depth styleguide along with presentations and videos conveyed what the company was aiming for. Studying all resources helped me continue their vision and suggest improvements where necessary.

Getting to Know the Users

With personas already established, it was my turn to dig deeper into user needs. As most of the interviewees had been using the first version of the product, I was able to observe and inquire about how they were using it.

With tailored scripts in hand, I conducted 20+ interviews to understand:

  • The user’s role at work
  • The user’s workflows
  • The user’s painpoints with work + product

RESEARCH TO DESIGN

Gathering Feedback

While maintaining that it was important to be aware of the company’s short-term vs. long-term goals, I gathered lists of 3-5 most important feedback/painpoints. Additionally, I looked for patterns across users.

Team Feedback

User feedback translated into mockups and meetings with the product head, design head, and CEO to determine the best course of action, eventually leading to design approval to produce assets + specifications for the dev team.

My Other Roles

I designed weekly emails that helped users keep track of their goals, new sign-up/sign-in flows, consulted company on the GUI of admin interface, and continually advised on best UX practices.

WHAT I LEARNED

Things change; adapting and being flexible is part of the role.

When in doubt, look at the data and research.

Having relationships with users is a good thing. You might need to talk to them again, so be pleasant, be polite, and make it fun.

Get to know your dev team. They have great insight. Share your process with them as well.

It’s an ongoing, iterative process. Keep moving!
